--- Lineage Overview Summary ---
Lineage Overview looks at the family history of Banu Hashim. This week, we look at Ja'far, the son of Ali and Fatima bint Asad. We also look at the relationship between the Prophet and his family.

--- Conversion and Early Islam Summary ---
Ja'far's father is Abu Talib and his mother is Fatima bint Asad. Some reports say he was as early as the 15th or 16th person to accept Islam. He and his wife Asma’ bint Umais accepting Islam together.

--- Persecution Summary ---
We don't have much about their life before the migration to Abyssinia, but we do know that they were amongst the family of the Prophet  and they were in that situation, subjected to similar persecution. And that the persecution became  severe for Ja'far and Asma’, that they sought permission to be amongst those who would go out to Abyssinian.

--- Hijra Summary ---
Asma’ was among those who considered to make hijrah to Abyssinia with her son, Ja'far, and with her daughter-in-law. They were amongst the family of the Prophet  and they were in that situation, subjected to similar persecution.

--- Battle Summary ---
Ja'far is among the first Muslims who would be martyred in battle as a commander. He is distinguished, he sends in that battle the first commander Zayd ibn Haritha, the beloved one of the Prophet. He saw Waraqa, he saw Zaid ibn Amir as we spoke about, and these were people he saw in Jannah.

--- Virtue(s) Summary ---
Ja'far ibn Abi Talib was one of the first Muslims to convert to Islam. He was part of the  first hijrah to Abyssinia which is modern day Ethiopia. Ja'far was martyred in the battle of Mu'tah.

--- Death Summary ---
Al-Abbas was among the first Muslims who would be martyred in battle as a commander, which we'll see in the battle of Mu'tah. He was also someone who was in a better financial state, who was skilled in his trade and often is referenced in regard to his successful business endeavours.
