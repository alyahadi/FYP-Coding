--- Lineage Overview Summary ---
Tahlah was born into a wealthy tribe, and that tribe is known as Banu Taym. He was married to several women of high lineage. He is connected to multiple tribes in Mecca from a very young age. Talhah is the person that would accompany the family of Abu Bakr to Medina on the Hijrah.

--- Conversion and Early Islam Summary ---
Talhah is considered the eighth person or one of the first eight to accept Islam at the age of 18 years old. He is the son of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq, who was the first Muslim to convert to Islam. Talhah was born into a family of Muslim converts.

--- Persecution Summary ---
Naufal ibn Khuwaylid tied Abu Bakr and Talhah up with one rope, and he violently tortured them. He also brought others to violently beat them while they were tied together by their hands and by their feet. Abu Bakr was initially tortured and punished by his mother As-Safiyyah bint Abdul Muttalib.

--- Hijra Summary ---
Talhah met the Prophet and Abu Bakr, who was his father-in-law, on the way out of Mecca, and he was coming back from Ash-Sham. Talhah is the person that would accompany the family of Abu Bakr to Medina on the Hijrah.

--- Battle Summary ---
Talhah and Sa'eed ibn Zayd were sent on a mission to spy on one of the caravans of Abu Sufyan. While they were out looking for this particular caravan, they heard about the battle of Badr that had already taken place while they were on their way back. Talhah is one of those people who missed Badr, and who longed for the moment to fight alongside the Prophet.

--- Virtue(s) Summary ---
Talhah was a man of extreme courage. The Prophet called him Talhah the good one. He was more generous with his money, with his clothes and with his food. He is considered the eighth person to accept Islam at the age of 18.

--- Death Summary ---
Talhah was one of the key warriors of Abu Bakr Al-Siddiq in the battle against Musaylimah Al-Kazzab. Because of his generosity, and because of his goodness, the Prophet  gave him three nicknames beyond “the living shaheed”
