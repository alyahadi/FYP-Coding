--- Lineage Overview Summary ---
Talhah was born into a wealthy tribe, and that tribe is known as Banu Taym. He was married to several women of high lineage. He is connected to multiple tribes in Mecca from a very young age. Talhah is the person that would accompany the family of Abu Bakr to Medina.

--- Conversion and Early Islam Summary ---
Talhah is considered the eighth person or one of the first eight to accept Islam. He now follows the man from Banu Hashim. Talhah accepts Islam alongside Abu Bakr as-Siddiq, and he is considered to be a follower of the Prophet.

--- Persecution Summary ---
Naufal ibn Khuwaylid tied Abu Bakr and Talhah up with one rope, and he violently tortured them. He also brought others to violently beat them while they were tied together by their hands and by their feet. After they had already left Mecca, he also informed them of some of the ways in which Medina was awaiting the Prophet.

--- Hijra Summary ---
Talhah met the Prophet and Abu Bakr, who was his father-in-law, on the way out of Mecca, and he was coming back from Ash-Sham. Talhah is the person that would accompany the family of Abu Bakr to Medina on the Hijrah.

--- Battle Summary ---
Talhah and Sa'eed ibn Zayd were on a mission to spy on one of the caravans of Abu Sufyan. They heard about the battle of Badr that had already taken place while they were on their way back. Talhah is one of those people who missed Badr, who longed for the moment to fight alongside the Prophet.

--- Virtue(s) Summary ---
Talhah was a man of extreme courage. The Prophet called him Talhah the good one. He was more generous with his money, with his clothes and with his food. He is considered the eighth person or one of the first eight to accept Islam.

--- Death Summary ---
Talhah was one of the key warriors of Abu Bakr Al-Siddiq in the battle against Musaylimah Al-Kazzab. Because of his generosity, and because of his goodness, the Prophet  gave him three nicknames beyond “the living shaheed”
